
# CYB/545 – Threat Intelligence

This repository documents the threat intelligence process through a series of analytical reports focused on Equifax Inc., a high-profile target in the financial data industry. Unlike traditional lab-based coursework, this class emphasizes research, risk evaluation, and professional-grade reporting aligned with industry frameworks.

---

## Deliverables

### Week 1 – Organizational Profile  
- Overview of Equifax’s structure, cybersecurity programs, and critical assets  
- Summary of the 2017 breach and post-incident recovery efforts  
- Establishes the foundation for contextual threat analysis

### Week 2 – Threat Landscape and Risk Assessment  
- Identification of internal and external cyber risks  
- Qualitative risk matrix using impact/likelihood modeling  
- Evaluation of Equifax’s current security posture and pressures

### Week 4 – Threat Analysis Data Collection  
- Breakdown of data types and collection criteria  
- Data reliability evaluation and operational security limitations  
- Tools, storage, validation, and normalization methods

### Week 6 – Intelligence-Sharing Findings  
- Summary of tests performed during breach investigation  
- Risk levels mapped to TLP (Traffic Light Protocol)  
- SOC staffing recommendations  
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework alignment  
- Actionable mitigation strategies including MFA, patching, and training

---

## Strategic Tools and Models Referenced

- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)**  
- **Traffic Light Protocol (TLP)**  
- **Risk Matrices (Impact vs. Likelihood)**  
- **Threat Intelligence Platforms (TIPs)**  
- **Open Source and Proprietary Threat Feeds**

---

## Value of the Course

CYB/545 prepares students for real-world threat intelligence work by focusing on data-driven decision making, actionable reporting, and strategic analysis. These deliverables reflect industry-level assessments and are formatted to align with GitHub portfolio expectations and professional cybersecurity communication.
