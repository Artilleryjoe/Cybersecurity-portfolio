## Organization Overview

**Equifax Inc.** is one of the three largest consumer credit reporting agencies in the United States, alongside Experian and TransUnion. Headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, Equifax operates or has investments in 24 countries across North America, Central and South America, Europe, and the Asia Pacific region.

Equifax collects and aggregates information on over 800 million individual consumers and more than 88 million businesses worldwide. The company offers a broad range of services, including credit reporting, fraud prevention, data analytics, employment verification, and identity protection.

---

## Organizational Structure

Equifax is structured as a public, multinational corporation with an executive leadership team and board of directors overseeing global operations. Its divisions include:

* U.S. Information Solutions (USIS)
* Workforce Solutions
* Global Consumer Solutions
* International
* Enterprise Risk and Compliance
* Global Technology and Cybersecurity

The **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** plays a central role in guiding cyber risk strategy and threat intelligence integration.

---

## Technological Infrastructure

Equifax maintains a hybrid technology environment with significant investment in:

* Cloud infrastructure (primarily via Amazon Web Services)
* On-premise data centers for legacy systems
* High-throughput analytics platforms
* Web applications and APIs for consumer access and financial clients
* Mobile applications with integrated identity verification

Security technologies include:

* Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)
* Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)
* Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)
* Data Loss Prevention (DLP)
* Vulnerability Management Tools

---

## Security Programs and Capabilities

Following its 2017 breach, Equifax underwent major security program overhauls:

* Adoption of a **Zero Trust Architecture**
* Deployment of **automated patch management**
* Redesign of third-party risk management
* Use of **Red and Blue Teams** for ongoing offensive and defensive readiness
* Real-time analytics for insider threat detection
* Integrated threat intelligence feeds from ISACs and commercial providers

Equifax collaborates with:

* The Financial Services Information Sharing and Analysis Center (FS-ISAC)
* U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS)
* External threat intelligence vendors and consultants

---

## Critical Assets and Data

Key information assets protected by Equifax include:

* Personally Identifiable Information (PII): Social Security Numbers, full names, birth dates
* Financial data: Credit histories, scores, and payment behavior
* Employment records: Payroll and tax data through The Work Number
* Partner and third-party integrations with financial institutions

These assets are high-value targets for nation-state actors, financially motivated cybercriminals, and malicious insiders.

---

## Threat History and Risk Landscape

In 2017, Equifax suffered one of the largest data breaches in U.S. history. A vulnerability in Apache Struts left an internet-facing web application unpatched, allowing attackers to exfiltrate personal data of approximately 147 million consumers.

Key contributing factors:

* Failure to apply known security patches in a timely manner
* Inadequate network segmentation
* Lack of visibility into unauthorized activity

Post-breach, Equifax made a \$1.4 billion investment into cybersecurity and IT modernization. This included leadership changes, cultural reforms, and a comprehensive risk management revamp.

Current threat intelligence indicates persistent risks:

* Phishing campaigns targeting customer service portals
* Credential stuffing attacks against web applications
* Third-party risk exposure via integrations
* Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) group interest in financial data
