# Cyber Ethics – Legal and Ethical Foundations in Cybersecurity

This folder contains course notes, essays, and key takeaways from the Cyber Ethics course. While the course does not include technical labs, it provides essential grounding in the legal, regulatory, and moral considerations that govern cybersecurity practices.

---

## Topics Covered

- **Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA)**
- **Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)**
- **Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)**
- **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**
- **Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)**
- **Cyberbullying, cyberstalking, and harassment laws**
- **Intellectual property in the digital age**
- **Professional ethics and responsibilities**
- **Whistleblower protections and disclosure**
- **Ethical hacking and boundaries of red teaming**

---

## Course Focus

Cyber Ethics explores the tension between technological innovation and ethical responsibility. It emphasizes the importance of:
- Legal compliance in cybersecurity operations  
- Respecting user privacy and data ownership  
- Evaluating ethical dilemmas in offensive and defensive security  
- Understanding the global landscape of cyber law
