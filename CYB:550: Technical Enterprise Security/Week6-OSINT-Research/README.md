## Slide 2 – What Is OSINT?

**Definition**: OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) refers to the collection and analysis of publicly available data to support decision-making, cybersecurity, and risk management.
**Sources Include**: Websites, news articles, forums, social media, search engines, public records, etc.

---

## Slide 3 – OSINT in Cybersecurity

**Uses**:

* Identify vulnerabilities in digital infrastructure
* Assess external risks and emerging threats
* Monitor competitor activity and technical disclosures
* Support incident response and threat intelligence

---

## Slide 4 – Target Organization: IBM

**Why IBM?**

* Global technology and consulting leader
* Major investments in AI, cloud, and cybersecurity
* A common target for cyber adversaries due to large attack surface

---

## Slide 5 – Tools Used for OSINT Collection

* **BuiltWith**: To analyze IBM's web technologies
* **Wappalyzer**: For insights into frameworks and platforms used
* **Google Dorking**: To identify exposed documents or directories
* **Social Media**: For tracking public perception and announcements

---

## Slide 6 – OSINT Findings

* IBM is actively investing in **quantum computing** and **AI security**
* Several IBM subdomains running on legacy tech
* High volume of technical whitepapers and blogs – reveals innovation trends
* Partnerships with Red Hat and other open-source communities

---

## Slide 7 – Competitive Intelligence Insights

* OSINT reveals IBM's **core differentiators** compared to AWS, Microsoft
* Identified customer sectors: government, finance, healthcare
* Open reports show emphasis on **Zero Trust** and **Hybrid Cloud** models

---

## Slide 8 – Recommendations for IBM

* Regular audit of exposed assets via OSINT tools
* Monitor threat actor chatter about IBM in forums and dark web
* Integrate OSINT feeds into SOC workflow
* Use sentiment analysis to anticipate reputational risk

---

## Slide 9 – Conclusion

* OSINT provides actionable intelligence for cybersecurity teams
* Public data can reveal both threats and opportunities
* Organizations like IBM benefit from proactive OSINT programs
